summary

This paper combines gravitational-wave luminosity distances with fast radio burst dispersion measures to constrain cosmological parameters without relying on redshift measurements.

Each observable alone is insufficient, but together they reduce degeneracy in the parameter space (e.g., Ωₘ, H₀), especially with future detectors like Cosmic Explorer.
